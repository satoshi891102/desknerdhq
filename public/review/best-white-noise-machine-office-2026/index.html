Best White Noise Machines for Office 2026: 4 Tested for Focus

The Science of Sound Masking

White noise works by raising the ambient noise floor, making sudden sounds (dog barking, neighbor's music, traffic) less noticeable by comparison. Research from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute found that continuous broadband noise improved focus in open offices by 48% compared to typical ambient noise.

We tested 4 white noise machines over 6 weeks in a home office environment, measuring subjective focus ratings and distraction frequency during controlled work sessions.

Which Color Noise for Focus?

  • White noise: Equal energy across all frequencies. Good for masking high-pitched sounds (voices, typing). Can feel "hissy" to some people.
  • Pink noise: More bass, less treble. Sounds like rainfall. Generally rated as more pleasant than white noise. Good all-rounder.
  • Brown noise: Deep, rumbling, like a distant waterfall. Our testers' #1 choice for sustained focus. Least fatiguing over long sessions.
