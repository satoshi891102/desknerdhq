2. Bose QC Ultra ‚Äî Best Comfort

If comfort is your #1 priority ‚Äî and for 8-hour days, it should be high on the list ‚Äî the Bose QC Ultra is unmatched. They're lighter than the Sony (250g vs 254g), but the real difference is ear cup design. Bose's oval cups fit a wider range of ear shapes without any pressure points.

ANC is excellent, just a hair behind Sony. Call quality matches the Sony. Where Bose loses points is battery life (24 hours vs Sony's 30) and the app experience, which is less polished.

3. Apple AirPods Max 2 ‚Äî Best Ecosystem

If you're all-in on Apple, the AirPods Max 2 are compelling. The seamless switching between Mac, iPhone, and iPad is genuinely magical ‚Äî no headphone does this better. Call quality is the best we tested, with Apple's computational audio creating crystal-clear voice isolation.

The downside: weight. At 384g, they're significantly heavier than competitors. After 5 hours, most testers needed a break. At $549, they're also the most expensive option.

4. Jabra Evolve2 85 ‚Äî Best for Calls

The Jabra is purpose-built for office communication. It comes with a USB dongle for zero-lag connectivity, a physical mute button on the ear cup (invaluable for Zoom calls), and the best microphone isolation in our test. Background noise virtually disappears for your call partners. ANC is weaker than Sony/Bose, but if calls are 50%+ of your day, this is the pick.

How We Tested

  • ANC effectiveness: Measured dB reduction in a controlled environment with simulated office noise (60 dB mix of HVAC, typing, conversation)
  • Call quality: Recorded 10 test calls per headphone, rated by 5 listeners on clarity, background noise, and natural sound
  • Comfort: 4 testers wore each headphone for full 8-hour work days, rating comfort at 2, 4, 6, and 8 hours
  • Battery life: Measured actual battery life with ANC on, 50% volume, mixed music and calls

The Sony WH-1000XM6 wins our top pick for overall office use. The Bose QC Ultra is the comfort king. If you're on Zoom/Teams all day, the Jabra Evolve2 85 is purpose-built for that. Skip the AirPods Max unless you're deep in the Apple ecosystem and don't mind the weight.
