Standing desk converters are the compromise solution ‚Äî and sometimes compromises are exactly right. You keep the desk you already have, you avoid a 2-hour assembly project, and you gain the ability to stand while working for a fraction of the cost of a full electric desk.

The question is: do they actually work well enough to use daily? Or do they become expensive monitor stands that never leave the sitting position? We tested 6 converters over 2 months to find out.

When a converter makes sense: You like your current desk. You rent and can't leave furniture behind. You want to test standing before committing to a $600+ desk. You need zero-assembly (some converters literally have no setup).

When a full desk makes sense: You need a clean desk surface for drawing/writing. You have 3+ monitors. You want height memory presets. You plan to stand for 4+ hours daily.

How We Tested

Each converter was placed on a standard 60x30" desk and used as a primary workstation for 2 weeks. We tested with a 27" monitor, a laptop, keyboard, and mouse ‚Äî a typical dual-screen setup. We measured:

  • Transition effort ‚Äî How hard is it to go from sitting to standing? Some use spring-assist, others use gas lifts, others require manual lifting.
  • Stability at standing height ‚Äî Does it wobble when typing? Does the monitor shake?
  • Usable workspace ‚Äî How much actual desk space do you have in standing position?
  • Keyboard height ergonomics ‚Äî Can you achieve proper typing height (elbows at 90¬∞)?
  • Daily use compliance ‚Äî Did we actually use it standing, or did it stay in sitting position?

Who Should Buy a Converter

  • Renters who don't want to buy and move a full desk
  • Standing-curious people who want to test before committing $600+
  • Minimal setup people who want zero or near-zero assembly
  • Budget-constrained buyers who need sit-stand for under $300

Skip converters if: You already know you'll stand 4+ hours daily (get a full desk), you need clean desk aesthetics (converters add visual bulk), or you have 3+ monitors (not enough surface area).
