Best Desktop Speakers for Office 2026: 5 Tested for Calls and Music

By DeskNerdHQ Team Updated 2026-02-13 12 min read

When Headphones Aren't the Answer

Headphones are great for noise cancellation, but wearing them 8 hours a day causes ear fatigue, heat, and social isolation (if you share your home). A good pair of desktop speakers gives you ambient music for focus, clear speakerphone for calls, and the freedom of not having something clamped to your head all day.

We tested 5 desktop speaker systems for the specific dual role of office music and video calls. Most speaker reviews focus on audiophile music quality ‚Äî we focused on how they perform in a real work environment.

2. Bose Companion 2 III ‚Äî Best for Calls

The Bose Companion 2 III won't blow you away with music quality, but they're the most versatile office speakers we tested. Clear dialogue reproduction makes calls sound natural, and the compact form factor takes minimal desk space. At $100, they're good value for a reliable daily driver.

3. Creative Pebble V3 ‚Äî Best Budget

At $35, the Pebble V3 punches well above its weight. USB-C powered (no wall adapter needed), with a surprisingly full sound for the size. They won't fill a room, but at desk distance they provide pleasant music and clear call audio. The compact pebble design also looks great on a desk.
