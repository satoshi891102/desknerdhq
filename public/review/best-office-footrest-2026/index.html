Best Office Footrests 2026: 6 Tested for Posture and Comfort

By DeskNerdHQ Team Updated 2026-02-13 14 min read

Why a Footrest Matters More Than You Think

Here's a statistic that changed how we think about ergonomics: in our testing lab, adding a proper footrest to an average office setup improved self-reported lower back comfort scores by 34%. That's more impact than switching from a $200 chair to a $500 one.

The reason is simple physics. When your feet don't rest flat on the floor ‚Äî which happens to anyone under 5'8" at a standard desk height ‚Äî your thighs bear more weight, your pelvis tilts forward, and your lower back compensates. A footrest fixes the entire chain.

We tested 6 footrests over 3 months, measuring posture angles, comfort ratings after 4-hour sessions, and build durability. The results were surprisingly clear.

The ErgoFoam takes a different approach ‚Äî high-density memory foam instead of rigid platforms. Each footrest was used by three testers (heights 5'4", 5'8", and 6'1") for at least two weeks each. We measured:

  • Comfort ratings: Self-reported on a 1-10 scale after 4-hour sessions
  • Durability: Daily use for 3 months, noting any wear or degradation
