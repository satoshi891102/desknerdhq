Best Webcam Lighting 2026: 5 Lights That Make You Look Professional on Zoom

By DeskNerdHQ Team Updated 2026-02-14 12 min read

Lighting Is 80% of How You Look on Camera

Your webcam doesn't matter nearly as much as your lighting. A $30 webcam with proper lighting will produce a better image than a $200 webcam in a dark room. 2. BenQ ScreenBar Halo ‚Äî Best Dual Purpose

The ScreenBar Halo serves double duty: it's a task light for your desk AND a bias light for your monitor. For video calls, the front-facing light illuminates your face while the back-facing LEDs reduce eye strain. The wireless controller puck makes adjustments effortless.

At $130, it's the most expensive option, but it replaces two products (desk lamp + video light). If you need a desk light anyway, the Halo is excellent value.

3. Neewer Ring Light 10" ‚Äî Best Budget

At $25, the Neewer ring light is impossible to beat on value. The 10" diameter provides even illumination, and the characteristic ring-light "catch light" in your eyes looks polished on camera. The tripod is adjustable from 11" to 18".

Downsides: the color accuracy isn't great (slight green tint at cooler temperatures), and the plastic construction feels cheap. But for $25, it transforms video call quality dramatically.

Video Call Lighting Tips

  • Light should face you, not behind you. A window behind you creates a silhouette. A window in front of you is free, perfect lighting.
  • Position light slightly above eye level. This mimics natural overhead sunlight and is universally flattering.
  • Match color temperature to your room. If your room has warm (yellow) lighting, set your video light to 3200K. Cool (white) room? Set to 5000K+. Mismatched temps look unnatural.
  • Avoid overhead-only lighting. It creates dark eye shadows ("raccoon eyes"). Always supplement with a front-facing light.
