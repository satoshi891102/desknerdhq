If you work from a laptop, a USB-C dock transforms your experience. Sit down, plug in one cable, and your laptop connects to: external monitors, ethernet, audio, USB peripherals, SD cards, and charging ‚Äî all through that single cable. Stand up, unplug one cable, and you're mobile. No reconnecting 6 cables every time.

The problem is that the USB-C dock market is a minefield of compatibility issues, thermal throttling (where the dock gets hot and reduces performance), and confusing port specifications. A dock that works perfectly with a MacBook might not support dual monitors on a Dell. A dock that handles 4K@60Hz on one port might only do 4K@30Hz on the second.

We tested 8 docking stations for three months. Our test covered: initial setup complexity, multi-monitor support (4K@60Hz on each), data transfer speeds (SSD benchmark via each USB port), thermal behavior (surface temperature after 8 hours), laptop charging reliability, and sleep/wake behavior (the silent killer ‚Äî many docks lose connection when the laptop sleeps).
